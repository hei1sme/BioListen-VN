### **NÔNG NGHIỆP**

**Biodiversity monitoring through Ecological acoustics**  
Context: Vietnam has some of Southeast Asia's richest national parks and biosphere reserves, but species inventories still rely on camera traps and manual surveys. No large-scale AI system for analyzing environmental audio yet exists in Vietnam. Requirements: Build a model that classifies animal calls (birds, frogs/amphibians, primates, insects) from continuous 24/7 audio captured by devices placed in the forest. The system must separate target species' sounds from background rain/wind/insect noise, detect chainsaw or hunting-rifle sounds (illegal logging/poaching alerts), and estimate an ecosystem-health index over time. Constraint: devices run on battery/solar and must process at the edge to reduce data transmission.

**AI Platform supporting agricultural management**  
Context  
Điện Biên is a mountainous province with a significant agricultural sector. Its staple crops include Điện Biên rice, Mường Ảng coffee, and winter-season vegetables. Agricultural officials currently collect information manually and lack analytical tools to detect disease early, forecast yields, and advise on optimal harvest and cultivation plans. Quarter-over-quarter or year-over-year comparison reports are produced manually, which is time-consuming and prone to missing important trends.

Problem to Solve  
The goal is to build an AI application supporting officials and farmers across four functions:

* Crop disease detection. A farmer photographs a leaf or stem using their phone. The AI then analyzes the image, identifies the disease or pest present, and proposes concrete treatment measures.  
* Yield forecasting and harvest planning. Based on cultivated area, crop variety, weather conditions, and seasonal history, the AI produces a yield forecast and recommends the optimal harvest timing.  
* Market price analysis. Price data is integrated so that officials and farmers have a reliable reference point when deciding when to sell their produce.  
* Period-comparison dashboard. Management can view automated reports comparing yield, cultivated area, and disease incidence by quarter or against the same period in the previous year.

**Energy digital twin for industrial parks: load forecasting and optimal dispatch of rooftop solar and battery storage toward green industrial park certification**  
Context: New-generation FDI tenants (electronics, semiconductors, textiles exporting to Europe) increasingly require industrial parks to supply clean energy to meet RE100 commitments and the EU carbon border mechanism (CBAM) — renewable-supply capacity is becoming a direct competitive factor in attracting investment. VRG's parks have huge rooftop-solar potential across hundreds of hectares of factory roofs, plus opportunities from the direct power purchase mechanism (DPPA). But operation is complex: load across hundreds of factories fluctuates by production shift and order season; solar output varies with weather; and decisions on battery investment, capacity allocation between tenants, and DPPA bidding require multi-objective stochastic optimization the Group currently handles only with spreadsheets. Requirements: Build a digital twin of the park's power grid that: forecasts each tenant's load and solar output hourly; optimizes real-time dispatch across generation, storage, and grid purchase; simulates investment scenarios (battery size, DPPA price); and automatically calculates renewable-energy certification per tenant to support eco-industrial-park documentation.Requirements: Build an AI system that learns from SCADA and monitoring data to: forecast input load 2–6 hours ahead; apply model-predictive control (MPC) or reinforcement learning to aeration, recirculation pumps, and chemical dosing; and give early warning of load-shock risk with response options — targeting a 15–25% electricity reduction while keeping effluent quality below discharge limits under all load scenarios.

### **ĐỔI MỚI SÁNG TẠO**

**Revenue Brain**  
Context: Large hotel chains have sophisticated revenue-management systems, while tens of thousands of homestays, guesthouses, and small tour companies in Vietnam price on gut feeling — leaving rooms empty in low season, selling cheap in peak season, and failing to anticipate visitor "waves." No AI pricing-forecast tool exists specifically for this micro segment.

Requirements: Build a lightweight, easy-to-use system for small-property owners that does two things: (1) forecast demand weekly/monthly for each property, combining seasonality, local festival/event calendars, weather, airfare, search trends, and regional competitor pricing; (2) recommend pricing and channel allocation — how much to raise/lower prices, which channels to prioritize, when to run promotions — with clear explanations so that people with no financial background still trust and follow them. Challenge: each small property has very little historical data of its own, requiring transfer/pooled learning across similar properties (cold-start).

Judging criteria: Demand-forecast accuracy (especially for data-poor properties), estimated revenue uplift in simulation versus gut-feel pricing, quality of the recommendation explanations, and ease of use for non-expert owners.

**Real-time AI optimal control of centralized industrial wastewater treatment plants**  
Context: Each VRG park runs a centralized wastewater plant of tens of thousands of m³/day on activated-sludge technology, receiving effluent from hundreds of factories with pollution loads that swing sharply hour-to-hour and unpredictably (especially during sudden discharges). Operation is currently experience-based: aeration blowers — 50–60% of the plant's total electricity — often run over-capacity "to be safe," and chemicals are dosed at fixed thresholds. The result is electricity and chemical costs billions of VND higher than necessary each year system-wide, while still risking load shocks that kill the microbial sludge (days to recover) and over-limit discharges that draw fines. This is a genuinely hard optimal-control problem: nonlinear biological process, long lag, continuous input noise. Requirements: Build an AI system that learns from SCADA and monitoring data to: forecast input load 2–6 hours ahead; apply model-predictive control (MPC) or reinforcement learning to aeration, recirculation pumps, and chemical dosing; and give early warning of load-shock risk with response options — targeting a 15–25% electricity reduction while keeping effluent quality below discharge limits under all load scenarios.

**Real-time urban flood forecasting & sponge-city drainage**  
Context: Cần Thơ's drainage infrastructure hasn't kept pace with rapid urbanization or the extreme-rainfall scenarios driven by climate change, and flood operations today rely mainly on experience without accurate forecasting or simulation. Requirements: Build a system that forecasts and simulates local flooding points in real time, at least 30–60 minutes ahead, and issues early warnings. It should include a multi-tier infrastructure architecture that optimizes storage-space capacity and drainage, a "sponge city" model adapted to Cần Thơ with a path to real-world deployment, and social-response governance plus self-draining construction materials that minimize flood impact. Expected products: a real-time flood monitoring/simulation/early-warning system, neighborhood-level real-time flood maps, smart drainage solutions, giant integrated underground water-storage tanks in multi-use underground spaces, and traffic/community response procedures for flood events.

### **PHÒNG CHỐNG THIÊN TAI**

**Real-time emission-source identification & environmental recovery toward Net-zero**  
Context: Emissions from traffic, industry, construction, urban life, and craft villages place heavy pressure on Cần Thơ's environment, with many rivers and lakes suffering prolonged pollution. Monitoring today relies mainly on periodic inspections, and the monitoring network and data are fragmented, so polluting sources can't be precisely identified in real time. Requirements: Build a system that detects and precisely identifies pollution sources (water and air) in real time, plus technologies to reduce emissions, treat pollution, and restore degraded areas. It should incorporate market mechanisms and economic tools — such as carbon credits — to drive emission reduction and encourage participation, and economic models that tie environmental recovery to the creation of new green ecosystems.

Expected products: a real-time emission-source monitoring and identification system, pollution-control/recovery technologies, and new economic tools and models.
